Images

Inline images

Include images to your html-file:

<img src='<%= require("./images/icon.png") %>' alt='the sun'>

Here is another example using the srcset-attribute:

<img sizes='100vw'
srcset='
<%= require("./images/the_moon_w_300.jpg")%> 300w
<%= require("./images/the_moon_w_845.jpg")%> 845w,
<%= require("./images/the_moon_w_1199.jpg")%> 1199w'
src='<%= require("./images/the_moon_w_1750.jpg") %>'
alt='the moon'>

Background images

Webpack will automatically detect background images in your CSS-code. No special syntax needed:

background: url('../images/tick.png');

Variables

The webpackhtml-plugin lets you interpolate variables into your HTML code. The syntax looks like this:

My favourite number is <%= JSON.stringify(htmlWebpackPlugin.options.favouriteNumber) %>.

Output:
My favourite number is <%= JSON.stringify(htmlWebpackPlugin.options.favouriteNumber) %>.


Partials

Include partials:

<%= require(./partials/_footer.html') %>

(Depends on the `html-loader`.)
